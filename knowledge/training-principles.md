# Training Science Reference

Evidence-based reference for programming resistance training. Use to evaluate plans, suggest modifications, and answer training questions.

---

## 1. Volume Landmarks (Working Sets / Muscle Group / Week)

- **MEV** (Minimum Effective Volume): ~6-8 sets
- **MAV** (Maximum Adaptive Volume): ~12-20 sets
- **MRV** (Maximum Recoverable Volume): ~20-25 sets

**Weekly set targets** (ranges span beginner to advanced):
- Chest: 10-20 | Back: 10-20 | Shoulders (side/rear): 8-16
- Quads: 8-18 | Hamstrings: 6-12 | Glutes: 6-16
- Biceps: 6-14 | Triceps (direct): 6-12 | Calves: 8-16 | Abs: 6-12

Triceps get significant indirect volume from pressing. Count ~50% of chest/shoulder pressing sets toward triceps total.

---

## 2. Progressive Overload Strategies

**Beginner (0-12 months):** Linear progression -- add weight every session (2.5 kg upper, 5 kg lower). Deloads rarely needed.

**Intermediate (1-3 years):** Weekly progression. Double progression: hit top of rep range for all sets, then add weight and reset to bottom (e.g., 3x8-12 -> hit 3x12 -> add weight, restart at 3x8). Deload every 4-6 weeks.

**Advanced (3+ years):** Periodized blocks (accumulation then intensification, 3-6 weeks each). DUP: vary rep ranges within a week. Autoregulation via RIR (Reps In Reserve) or RPE; target RIR 1-3 for working sets, RIR 0 sparingly.

---

## 3. Rep Range Guidelines

| Goal | Reps | % 1RM | Rest |
|------|------|-------|------|
| Strength | 1-5 | >85% | 3-5 min |
| Hypertrophy | 6-12 | 65-85% | 1.5-3 min |
| Endurance | 12-20+ | <65% | 30-90 sec |

Hypertrophy occurs across all ranges near failure, but 6-12 is most time-efficient. Compounds benefit from heavier work (3-8 reps); isolations are safer and more effective at 8-15+. Most programs should mix ranges.

---

## 4. Common Imbalances and Corrections

- **Push:Pull** -- Target 1:1 to 1:1.5 favoring pulling. Prioritize rows, pull-ups, face pulls.
- **Anterior:Posterior** -- Balance quad volume with hamstring/glute volume. Add RDLs, hip thrusts, or leg curls if quad-dominant.
- **Upper:Lower** -- At least 30-40% of volume should be lower body. Flag upper-heavy programs.
- **Bilateral:Unilateral** -- If >80% bilateral, add unilateral work (Bulgarian split squats, single-arm rows, lunges) to fix asymmetries.

---

## 5. Split Recommendations by Frequency

**2 days:** Full Body x2. Simple, sufficient for beginners. Sessions may run long.

**3 days:** Full Body x3 (best for beginners/early intermediate) | PPL (more volume per session but only 1x/week frequency) | Upper/Lower/Full.

**4 days (recommended default):** Upper/Lower x2. Each muscle 2x/week -- best balance of volume, frequency, and recovery. Alt: PPL + Arms day.

**5-6 days:** PPL x2 (each muscle 2x/week, high volume). Arnold Split (Chest+Back / Shoulders+Arms / Legs, repeat). Demanding on recovery; requires good sleep and nutrition.

---

## 6. Recovery and Deload

- **Between sessions:** 48-72 hours rest per muscle group.
- **Sleep:** 7-9 hours/night. <6 hours impairs strength, protein synthesis, and injury risk.
- **Deload:** Every 4-8 weeks. Reduce volume 40-60% for 1 week (preferred) or reduce intensity 40-60%.
- **Overtraining signs:** Persistent fatigue 2+ weeks, declining performance, elevated resting HR, disrupted sleep, joint/tendon pain worsening over sessions, loss of motivation. Recommend deload before adding volume.

---

## 7. Cardio Interference

- **Low-intensity** (walking, easy cycling): Minimal interference. Daily is fine.
- **Moderate** (jogging, steady-state): Low-moderate interference. 2-3x/week OK; separate from lifting by 3+ hours.
- **High-intensity** (HIIT, sprints): Moderate-high interference. Separate from leg training by 6+ hours; limit 1-2x/week.
- **>4 moderate+ sessions/week** may impair hypertrophy, especially lower body.
- Cycling interferes less than running (less eccentric damage).
- For fat loss, prioritize caloric deficit over excessive cardio.

---

## 8. RIR-Based Autoregulation

RIR (Reps In Reserve) = how many more reps you could perform. RPE 10 = RIR 0 (failure), RPE 8 = RIR 2, RPE 7 = RIR 3.

**Target RIR by level:** Beginner: RIR 3-4 (compounds), 2-3 (isolations). Intermediate: RIR 1-3. Advanced: RIR 1-3, RIR 0 sparingly on final sets.

**Set-to-set adjustment:** If actual RIR drops 2+ below target, reduce weight 5-10% for remaining sets. If RIR stays 2+ above target, increase weight 5% or add reps. A 1-2 RIR natural drift across sets is normal.

**Injury prevention:** Prescribe RIR 3-4 for exercises involving injured/recovering areas. If RIR hits 0-1 unexpectedly, reduce load — do not push through.

See coaching-guidelines.txt for full autoregulation rules.

---

## 9. 1RM Estimation

**Epley:** 1RM = weight x (1 + reps / 30)
**Brzycki:** 1RM = weight x (36 / (37 - reps))

Only reliable for sets of <10 reps. Epley slightly overestimates; Brzycki is more conservative. Re-estimate every 4-8 weeks.

**Reps to % 1RM:** 1 rep = 100% | 3 = 93% | 5 = 87% | 8 = 80% | 10 = 75% | 12 = 70% | 15 = 65%

---

## 10. Biometric Considerations

**Max HR estimation:** 220 - age (Haskell/Fox formula). Approximation with standard deviation of ~10-12 bpm. User-measured max HR is always preferred.

**Relative strength benchmarks** (1RM / bodyweight):

| Lift | Beginner | Intermediate | Advanced |
|------|----------|-------------|----------|
| Squat | 0.75x | 1.25x | 1.75x |
| Deadlift | 1.0x | 1.5x | 2.25x |
| Bench Press | 0.5x | 1.0x | 1.5x |
| Overhead Press | 0.35x | 0.65x | 1.0x |

Approximate male standards. Female standards are typically 60-75% of these values for upper body and 80-90% for lower body. Use for context only — never as targets the user must hit.

**Age and recovery:** Recovery capacity generally decreases with age. After 40, consider extending inter-session recovery for the same muscle group from 48h to 72h. After 60, prioritize movement quality and joint health over load progression.

**BMI formula:** weight_kg / (height_m)^2. BMI is a population-level screening tool, not an individual body composition measure. Body fat % (from DEXA, calipers, or bioimpedance) is more informative for training decisions.
